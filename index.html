What is the Retail Aggregation Program?

The Retail Aggregation Program (RAP) allows a group of end-users within a specific area to pool their electricity demand to meet the 500kW minimum requirement to be part of the Contestable Market. This gives them the power to choose their own Retail Electricity Supplier (RES).

By combining their demand, smaller businesses and consumers can gain access to competitive electricity prices and services that were previously only available to large industrial or commercial users. This program is designed to promote consumer choice, encourage competition among electricity suppliers, and ultimately lead to better rates and services for more Filipinos.

An Aggregator, who is a licensed RES, acts on behalf of the group to negotiate with power generators and manage their electricity supply contract.

Making the Switch to a Retail Supplier

Step 1: Form a Group and Choose an Aggregator

Gather interested members in your contiguous area and choose an ERC-licensed Retail Aggregator. The aggregator will guide you through the process and represent your group.

Step 2: Notification and Contract

Your chosen aggregator will notify your Distribution Utility (DU) at least 60 days before the switch. During this time, you will finalize and sign a Retail Supply Contract (RSC) with your aggregator.

Step 3: Meter Installation and Finalization

Your DU has up to 30 days to install the required interval meters for all members of the group. Once meters are installed and the contract is finalized, your aggregator will manage the switch with the Central Registration Body.

Step 4: Enjoy the Benefits

Once the switch is complete, you will begin receiving electricity under your new competitive price plan. Your aggregator will handle billing and any coordination needed with the supplier and DU.

Meter Types for Retail Competition

Cumulative Meter

If your business is currently buying electricity from your Distribution Utility (DU) at the regulated tariff, you are likely using a cumulative meter. Your DU sends meter readers to your premises to record your total electricity usage for the billing period (e.g., monthly). This type of meter is not sufficient for participating in retail choice programs.

Advanced Meter (Interval Meter)

Advanced meters, also known as smart meters or part of an Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI), are **mandatory** for participation in the Retail Aggregation Program. These meters allow electricity consumption to be measured in short intervals (typically 5-minute intervals in the Philippines), providing the detailed data necessary for accurate billing and settlement in the competitive electricity market.

There are two types of advanced meters: single-phase and three-phase. The type of meter required for your premises depends on your voltage intake level. Your Distribution Utility is responsible for installing the appropriate meter within 30 days of your request, and the cost is typically shouldered by the end-user.

What is a Load Profile?

The data from an advanced meter is used to create a "load profile" for your business or group. This is a graph that shows your electricity consumption pattern throughout the day. By understanding when you use the most electricity, Retail Electricity Suppliers can offer more customized and competitive price plans that are tailored to your specific usage patterns. This is not feasible with a cumulative meter which only shows a single consumption total for the month.
